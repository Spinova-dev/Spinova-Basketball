📋 Coach Note — Sporting (Mustafa Fikri)

This was a tale of two halves. Sporting were brilliant for the first half — Farida Sherif hit multiple 3-pointers, the double-screen system produced open shots, and the team's defensive intensity created 5 steals. Going in 7 points up at half-time felt deserved.

The foul problem returned as the decisive factor. Habiba Sorrow was benched with 3 fouls by Q2. Kanzi sat out the entire second quarter. The resulting loss of rotation depth meant Sporting were running on fumes in Q3 — a fresh Fatma Ahmed scored 8+ points while Sporting managed only 4. The 7-point lead became a draw and then a loss. Until foul discipline is solved, this team will keep losing close games.

📋 Coach Note — Smouha (Mohamed Yasser) — Champions

Smouha showed world-class adaptability. Down 7 at halftime with their top scorer (Basmala) in foul trouble, Coach Yasser made the pivotal decision: bring Fatma Ahmed back for Q3. The result was a 11-4 quarter that erased the deficit and changed the match completely.

5 Turning Points — Match 2
#1
Rawda's 3PT → First Sporting lead
With Smouha leading early, Rawda Osama hits a 3-pointer to give Sporting their first lead (18-17). This completely changed the psychological dynamic — showed Sporting they could win from behind.
📌 Lesson: Having multiple 3-point threats means you're never truly out of a game.
#2
Habiba's 3rd Foul in Q2 → Benched at 41-34
Habiba's 3rd foul forced her off with 7 minutes left in Q2. Combined with Kanzi sitting the entire quarter, Sporting played Q3 with half their starting lineup in foul trouble — directly causing the 4-point Q3 output.
📌 Lesson: Foul management in Q1-Q2 determines if you can run full rotation in Q3-Q4.
#3
Coach Yasser brings Fatma back for Q3
The tactical masterstroke. With Sporting's big defenders in foul trouble, bringing Fatma back in Q3 was a calculated exploitation. Sporting had no answer. This single substitution decision won Smouha the match.
📌 Lesson: Track the opponent's foul trouble in real-time and exploit their reduced rotation.
#4
Fatma's 3PT → 49-47 Smouha Q4
After the game was tied 47-47, Fatma Ahmed hits a 3-pointer — her new weapon — to take the lead for the first time since Q1. A center hitting a 3PT at a critical moment is impossible to defend against without prior scouting.
📌 Lesson: Develop your center's range shot — it changes the entire defensive calculus.
#5
Basmala's 5th Foul — 3 min left
Just like Sporting in Match 1, Smouha lost their key guard to fouling out in the 4th quarter. Unlike Match 1, Smouha had enough lead and enough Fatma to survive. But this is a serious warning — in a closer game, this would have been fatal.
📌 Lesson: The same foul problems that almost cost Smouha Match 1 nearly cost them Match 2 too.

Priority Training — 2-Match Evidence Base
🏋️ Sporting — Top 5 Priorities
Priority #1 — Foul footwork clinic: Habiba and Kanzi both commit fouls using the same reaching technique. Run weekly defensive footwork drills: slide feet, don't reach. Target: max 2 fouls per player per match.
Priority #2 — Habiba's free throws: 0/4 in Match 2, poor in Match 1. Practice FTs after running 5 sprints — replicate fatigue. Must reach 70%+ before next tournament.
Priority #3 — Q3 backup plan: What does Sporting do when Habiba/Kanzi are sitting? Develop Karma and Nour into primary scorers so Q3 isn't a dead quarter.
Priority #4 — Defending Fatma Ahmed: Scout Fatma specifically. Practice defending tall post players without fouling. Zone defense may be needed when Fatma is on court.
Priority #5 — Late-game composure: Both turnovers (Rawda travel, Arwa fumble) happened under pressure. Practice late-game ball security: catch, pivot, look, pass — never rush.
🏋️ Smouha — Top 5 Priorities
Priority #1 — Basmala's FT shooting: 30% across 2 matches. This is the single biggest weakness of the champion team. Daily FT practice targeting 70%+. Use the BEEF technique — Balance, Eyes, Elbow, Follow-through.
