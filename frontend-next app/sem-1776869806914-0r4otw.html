📝 Match Narrative
First Quarter — Smouha Sets the Tone (17–14)

Smouha opened with aggressive interior play, converting 4 of their first 6 two-point attempts. Sporting responded with perimeter shooting but converted only 1 of 4 three-point attempts in the quarter. Smouha's defensive pressure forced 5 Sporting turnovers in the opening period, converting 6 points off those mistakes. Despite the deficit, Sporting showed resilience with strong free-throw shooting (4/5) to stay within striking distance.

Second Quarter — Sporting's Best Period (18–19)

Sporting's most competitive quarter saw them nearly match Smouha point-for-point. Improved ball movement generated 5 assists and cleaner looks from mid-range. Smouha maintained their edge through offensive rebounding, securing 4 second-chance opportunities that yielded 6 points. The halftime deficit stood at 32–36, a manageable gap heading into the break.

Third Quarter — Smouha Extends (16–18)

The third quarter proved decisive. Smouha's interior dominance continued as they outscored Sporting in the paint 10–6 during this period. Sporting's turnover issues resurfaced (6 in the quarter), and Smouha's transition offense capitalized with 5 fast-break points. Sporting's three-point shooting went cold (1/6), limiting their ability to close the gap. The quarter ended 48–54.

Fourth Quarter — Smouha Closes Out (15–16)

Sporting fought hard in the final period, cutting the deficit to 6 points midway through the quarter. However, Smouha's composure under pressure — converting 6/8 free throws in the final two minutes — sealed the victory. Sporting's late-game fouling strategy backfired as Smouha's free-throw accuracy held firm. Final score: 63–70.
